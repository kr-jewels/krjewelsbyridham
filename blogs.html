What is Anti-Tarnish Jewellery? Why It's Trending in 2025

Published by KR by Ridham | June 2025

Anti-tarnish jewellery is becoming a buzzword in the fashion and accessories world — and for good reason. Whether you're dressing up for a wedding, office, or daily outings, you want your jewellery to stay as brilliant as the day you bought it. This is where anti-tarnish jewellery steps in as a game-changer.

What is Anti-Tarnish Jewellery?

Anti-tarnish jewellery is specially coated to resist oxidation and corrosion, meaning it doesn't darken or lose its shine over time. The protective layer — often a micro-coating of lacquer, rhodium, or gold — acts as a barrier between the metal and moisture, sweat, and air.

Why is it Trending in 2025?

  • Longevity: Modern buyers want jewellery that lasts, even if it isn't made of pure gold or silver.
  • Affordability: Anti-tarnish pieces offer a luxurious look without the premium price tag.
  • Low Maintenance: No more polishing or cleaning every week.
  • Skin-Friendly: Most coatings are hypoallergenic, perfect for sensitive skin types.
  • Eco-Conscious Shoppers: With less need for chemical cleaning, these products are more sustainable.

How to Care for Anti-Tarnish Jewellery

Though they resist tarnish, these tips will keep your jewellery looking new:

  • Store them in a dry, airtight pouch or box.
  • Avoid contact with perfumes, lotions, and water when possible.
  • Wipe gently with a soft cloth after use.
